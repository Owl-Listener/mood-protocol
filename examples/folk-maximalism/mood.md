# Mood: Example Mood

## Essence
Handmade mythology for the modern screen. This is the energy of a linocut pulled from a warm press, creatures and symbols crowding the frame with the confidence of folk art that has been telling stories for centuries. Dense, graphic, unapologetically imperfect, and alive with the kind of character that only comes from the human hand.

## Colour
- **Temperature:** Warm neutral, anchored by natural substrates — kraft, bone, unbleached paper
- **Palette:**
  - #1a1a1a "block black" — the dominant force, ink-dense and decisive
  - #f5f0e6 "warm parchment" — the ground, not white, never clinical white
  - #c8533b "kiln red" — a terracotta accent, earthy and hot, used sparingly
  - #d4541e "folk orange" — a bolder citrus note appearing in select pieces, vibrant against black
  - #2a7a6a "patina teal" — rare, striking when it appears, like the green fox figure
- **Contrast:** Extremely high. This is a world of figure and ground, black on light, with almost no mid-tones. The contrast does the work that colour would do in another palette.
- **Saturation:** Mostly achromatic. When colour appears it is vivid and earthy — never synthetic, never pastel. The restraint makes the colour moments feel like events.

## Typography
- **Character:** Hand-lettered, block-cut, imperfect. Type behaves like illustration here — "EIGHT LEGS NO PROBLEM," "SCARE THEM WITH KINDNESS," "IT'S A MESS" are drawn, not set. Uppercase, condensed, often filling its container like a woodblock stamp.
- **Weight distribution:** Heavy. Everything leans bold. Thin weights would feel foreign here.
- **Density:** Packed. Letters crowd together the way the illustrations crowd the frame. No breathing room in the type, and that's intentional — it creates the same folk energy as the images.
- **Suggested pairings:** If translating to digital type, look for faces with visible hand-drawn DNA: GT Alpina, Recoleta, or a display serif like Fraunces for headers. For body, something warm and slightly irregular like Literata or Newsreader. Avoid anything geometric or constructed.

## Space & Layout
- **Rhythm:** Horror vacui — the fear of empty space. These compositions fill every corner. Creatures stack on creatures, symbols interlock, negative space is eaten by the next mark. The density IS the aesthetic.
- **Grid character:** Organic and broken. No grid visible. Compositions grow outward from a central form like organisms, or tile in irregular patterns like hand-printed wallpaper. Some pieces use rough symmetry, but it is the symmetry of a handmade thing, never mathematically precise.
- **Scale relationships:** Dramatic and playful. A large central creature surrounded by tiny symbols. A massive eye next to a miniature bird. Scale shifts feel like folklore — the important thing is biggest, the supporting cast fills the margins.

## Texture & Material
- **Surface quality:** Deeply tactile. Every surface carries the trace of its making — the grain of a woodblock, the pressure of a printing press, the tooth of rough paper. Nothing is smooth or digital.
- **Material references:** Linocut on kraft paper. Rubber stamps on newsprint. Screenprint on cotton. Mural paint on brick. The aesthetic of the hand-printed, the small-run, the artist's studio.
- **Finish:** Matte, always. The slight sheen of dried ink on absorbent paper. Never glossy, never backlit.

## Emotional Register
This feels like walking into a folk art market in a Scandinavian fishing village, or finding a box of hand-printed postcards in a secondhand bookshop. There is humour here — "Eight Legs, No Problem," creatures with crossed-out eyes, a monster smiling. There is darkness too — wolves, skulls, the macabre. But the darkness is playful, like a children's book about monsters written by someone who actually respects children's intelligence. The overall feeling is warmth and wildness, craft and irreverence. It says: I was made by a person who was enjoying themselves.

The phrases visible in the board — "scare them with kindness," "life is weird," "it's a mess" — carry a philosophy: embrace the chaos, find the humour in it, make something beautiful with rough hands.

## Design Principles
- **Fill the frame** — Density is generosity. More is more. Empty space feels sterile in this world.
- **Show the hand** — Imperfection is the signature. Wobbly lines, uneven ink, visible process. If it looks machine-made, it has failed.
- **Creatures over abstractions** — Communicate through beings, characters, animals, monsters. Abstract shapes serve the creatures, not the other way around.
- **Restrained colour, bold when it lands** — The palette is almost monochrome, which makes any colour a deliberate act. Use it like punctuation.
- **Dark humour, warm heart** — Skulls and sweetness coexist. The tone is wry, never cynical. "Scare them with kindness" is the ethos.

## References
- **Minna Leunig** — Visible as a named artist reference. Dense black-and-white creature compositions with Scandinavian folk art DNA. The most direct influence on the overall mood.
- **Enikő Katalin (GoodMood)** — Bold orange and black pieces with Eastern European folk art energy. The colour accent reference.
- **DROOL ART** — "Eight Legs No Problem" and "Scare Them With Kindness" pieces. Where the hand-lettering meets illustration, and where the humour lives.
- **Block print / linocut tradition** — Across almost every image. The production method IS the aesthetic.
- **Scandi Beasts (Creative Market)** — The commercial expression of this folk style, showing it can scale to products and patterns.
- **Mural and environmental applications** — Several images show this aesthetic at wall scale, on buildings, in living rooms. It wants to be big. It wants to fill a space.

## Anti-References
- Clean, geometric, Swiss-style design — anything Helvetica-adjacent
- Flat digital illustration with perfect vector curves
- Pastel palettes or gradient-heavy aesthetics
- Minimalism in the Scandinavian-modern sense (this IS Scandinavian, but the maximalist folk tradition, not the IKEA aisle)
- Anything that looks like it was made in Figma by default — rounded rectangles, drop shadows, blur effects
- Polished, corporate, "designed" feeling — this board is resolutely anti-polish

## Agent Instructions
When applying this mood, think handmade maximalism. Fill compositions densely — leave almost no white space. Use a strict palette of near-black (#1a1a1a) on warm off-white (#f5f0e6), with terracotta red (#c8533b) or folk orange (#d4541e) as rare, intentional accents. All type should feel hand-drawn or at minimum have visible character and irregularity — never use geometric sans-serifs. Favour heavy weights and uppercase for display text. Illustrations and decorative elements should feel like block prints: bold shapes, no gradients, visible texture if possible. When in doubt, add more density, more character, more creature. The worst mistake would be making something that feels clean and digital. This mood wants to feel like it was pulled from a press, not rendered on a screen. Embrace imperfection. Show the hand.
